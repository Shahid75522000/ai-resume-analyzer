Top ATS Rejection Reasons (And How to Fix Them)

More than 70% of resumes get rejected by ATS systems before a human sees them. Here’s why—and how to fix it.

ATS (Applicant Tracking System) is used by most companies to filter resumes. If your resume is not optimized, it may get rejected even if you’re highly skilled. Here are the most common ATS rejection reasons and how to fix each one.

---

1. Missing Keywords from the Job Description

ATS looks for specific keywords related to skills, tools and domain experience. If they’re missing, your resume gets rejected.

Fix:
  • Match keywords with the Job Description
  • Add relevant tools: Excel, SQL, Power BI, etc.
  • Add domain terms: SLA, AHT, Inventory, Sales KPIs
⭐ Use tools like “Find” (Ctrl + F) to compare your resume with the JD.
---

2. Using Images, Icons or Tables

ATS cannot read text inside images or complex tables. Many modern graphic templates get rejected immediately.

Fix:
  • Use simple text-based format
  • Use Word or PDF (text-based, not scanned)
  • Avoid fancy columns/icons
---

3. Wrong File Format

Some ATS tools struggle with:

  • Scanned PDFs
  • Image-based resumes
  • Complex fonts
Fix: Save as: .docx or text-based PDF.
---

4. Unclear Resume Structure

ATS reads resumes from top to bottom. If your headings are unclear, ATS cannot understand your sections.

Fix:
  • Use standard headings like “Experience”, “Education”, “Skills”
  • Avoid creative headings like “What I Do” or “My Journey”
---

5. Using Headers/Footers

ATS sometimes ignores text inside headers or footers. If your contact details are there, ATS may not detect them.

Fix:
  • Place contact info in the main body, not in header/footer
---

6. Too Many Graphics or Multi-column Layouts

ATS struggles with multiple columns because it reads left to right. Your key info may get lost.

Fix: Use a clean, single-column ATS-friendly layout.
---

7. Too Much Paragraph Text

Long paragraphs make it harder for ATS to detect keywords.

Fix:
  • Use bullet points
  • Start sentences with action verbs
  • Add metrics: “Reduced SLA breaches by 25%”
---

8. Missing Job Titles

If your resume does not clearly mention your job roles, ATS may not match you to the position.

Fix:
  • Use simple job titles like “MIS Executive”, “Data Analyst”, “BI Developer”
---

9. Incorrect or Unusual Fonts

ATS cannot read decorative or handwritten fonts.

Fix:
  • Use fonts like Calibri, Arial, or Times New Roman
---

10. No Action Verbs

Action verbs help ATS match your experience to requirements.

Examples:
  • Analyzed
  • Automated
  • Developed
  • Optimized
  • Designed
---

11. Resume Too Short or Too Long

  • Too Short → lacks keywords
  • Too Long → ATS may skip important text
Fix: 1–2 pages is ideal.
---

12. No Skills Section

ATS heavily relies on Skills section. Missing it = instant rejection.

Fix:
  • Create a dedicated Skills section
  • Include tools + analytics + soft skills
---

13. Missing Dates or Incomplete Experience

ATS cannot understand incomplete timelines like:

“2022 – Present (Data Analyst at XYZ)”

Fix:
  • Add MM/YYYY format
  • Example: Jan 2023 – Present
---
✔ Always tailor your resume to each job ✔ Use clean formatting ✔ Avoid images/icons ✔ Use job description keywords ✔ Test resume with an ATS checker (like your AI Resume Analyzer!)

Fixing these issues can instantly increase your ATS score and improve your chances of getting shortlisted for interviews.
