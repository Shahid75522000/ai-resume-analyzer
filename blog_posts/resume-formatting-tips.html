Best Resume Formatting Tips for 2026 (ATS-Friendly Guide)

Make your resume clean, professional and ATS-friendly with these proven formatting rules used by top recruiters.

Resume formatting plays a huge role in ATS shortlisting and recruiter decisions. Even if your skills are strong, a poorly formatted resume can reduce your selection chances. These formatting tips will ensure your resume looks modern, clean and professional.

---

1. Use a Clean, Single-Column Layout

Multi-column or graphic-heavy layouts can confuse ATS. A single-column format is best for readability and keyword scanning.

Recommended sections:
  • Header
  • Summary
  • Skills
  • Experience
  • Projects
  • Education
  • Certifications
---

2. Use Standard Headings

ATS understands standard headings. Avoid fancy labels like “My Journey” or “What I Do”.

Use these:
  • Experience
  • Education
  • Skills
  • Projects
---

3. Choose an ATS-Friendly Font

Avoid decorative fonts. Use:

  • Calibri
  • Arial
  • Verdana
  • Times New Roman
---

4. Use 10–12pt Font Size

Optimal sizes:

  • Heading: 14–16pt
  • Body text: 10–12pt
---

5. Limit Your Resume to 1–2 Pages

Keep it short, but complete. ATS prefers 1–2 pages maximum.

---

6. Use Bullet Points, Not Long Paragraphs

Bullets are easier for ATS to read and faster for recruiters to scan.

Example:
  • Automated daily MIS reports using Excel & Power Query
  • Improved data accuracy by 20% using validation rules
---

7. Include Numbers & Metrics

Numbers make your experience more impactful.

Example:
  • Reduced reporting time by 40%
  • Saved 6 hours weekly through automation
---

8. Avoid Icons, Images & Tables

ATS cannot read text inside icons, logos or complex table formatting.

Avoid:
  • Photo
  • Icons
  • Fancy templates
---

9. Use Consistent Alignment & Spacing

A clean resume has consistent line spacing (1–1.15) and evenly spaced sections.

---

10. Use Simple Bullet Style

Use simple, round bullets instead of decorative ones:

• Recommended

---

11. Separate Skills into Categories

Group skills to make your resume look organized.

Example:
  • Tools: Excel, SQL, Power BI
  • Analytics: Data Cleaning, KPI Analysis
  • Soft Skills: Communication, Problem Solving
---

12. Use Consistent Date Format

Choose one format and use it everywhere:

  • Jan 2023 – Present
  • 2020 – 2022
---

13. Save as PDF (But Text-Based)

Do not upload scanned PDF or photo-based resumes. Always export from Word or Google Docs.

---

14. Check ATS Compatibility

This will show missing keywords and formatting issues.
