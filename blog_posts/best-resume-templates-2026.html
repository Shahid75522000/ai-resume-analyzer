Best Resume Templates for 2026 (ATS-Friendly)

Choose a clean, modern, ATS-safe template that recruiters actually prefer in 2026.

There are thousands of fancy resume templates online—but many are not ATS-friendly. In 2026, recruiters and ATS systems prefer clean, simple layouts. Here are the best types of templates you can use.

1. Simple One-Column ATS Template

This is the safest and most recommended format. It has a single column with clear headings and no graphics.

Sections:
  • Header (Name, contact, LinkedIn)
  • Summary
  • Skills
  • Experience
  • Projects
  • Education

2. Hybrid Template (Skills + Experience)

This template highlights skills on top, followed by experience. Great for Data / MIS roles where tools matter.

Top part:
  • Summary
  • Key Skills (Tools, Analytics, Soft Skills)
Then:
  • Experience
  • Projects
  • Certifications

3. Template for Freshers

Fresher templates focus on:

  • Education
  • Projects
  • Internships
  • Skills

Keep it one page and highlight academic projects relevant to data/analytics.

4. What to Avoid in Templates

  • Profile photos
  • Icons and graphics
  • Two or three-column designs with text in shapes
  • Heavy colors and gradients
  • Text placed inside images

5. Must-Haves in a Good Template

  • Clear hierarchy (Headings → bullets)
  • Enough white space
  • Simple fonts (Calibri, Arial)
  • Consistent bullet styles
  • Exportable as PDF
