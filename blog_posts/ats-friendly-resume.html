How to Write an ATS-Friendly Resume (2026 Guide)

Make your resume pass Applicant Tracking Systems and increase your job shortlist chances instantly.

In 2025, over 92% of companies use ATS systems such as Naukri RMS, Workday, Zoho Recruit, and LinkedIn TalentHub to filter resumes before human review. If your resume is not ATS-friendly, it will not pass the first round — no matter how good your skills are.

What is ATS?

ATS (Applicant Tracking System) is software that scans resumes and compares them with the Job Description. It extracts:

  • Skills
  • Experience
  • Education
  • Keywords
  • Job titles

Your goal is to write a resume that matches the keywords ATS searches for.

1. Use a Simple ATS-Friendly Resume Format

ATS cannot read complicated layouts. Use a clean, text-based format.

Use:
  • Simple fonts (Arial, Calibri, Helvetica)
  • Single-column layout
  • Clear section headings (WORK EXPERIENCE, EDUCATION, SKILLS)
Avoid:
  • Multiple columns
  • Tables
  • Icons, images, logos
  • Colorful designs

2. Match Resume Keywords with the JD

ATS compares your resume with the job description. Your resume must contain the same words the JD uses.

Example:

If JD requires “Data Cleaning”, but your resume says “Data Preparation”, ATS might not match it.

Use the same keyword: Data Cleaning.

3. Use Proper Section Headings

ATS recognizes standard headings only. Use these:

  • Professional Summary
  • Skills
  • Work Experience
  • Projects
  • Education
  • Certifications
❌ Do NOT use fancy headings like: “What I Can Do”, “Career Story”, “My Journey” ATS will not understand them.

4. Use Numbers to Show Impact

ATS and recruiters both prefer measurable achievements.

Examples:
  • Improved report accuracy by 28%
  • Automated Excel tasks saving 10 hours/week
  • Handled 200+ customer queries daily

5. Keep It 1 Page (Max 2 for experienced)

ATS does not care about design — it cares about clarity. A 1-page resume is ideal for:

  • Fresher
  • 1–3 years experience
  • Career switchers

6. Save as PDF (Unless JD says DOCX)

Most ATS systems accept PDF. But some older ATS require DOCX.

Safe Rule: Submit PDF unless the job explicitly asks for DOCX.

Final Tips for ATS Success

✔ Use simple formatting ✔ Add keywords from JD ✔ Use action verbs (Managed, Built, Optimized) ✔ Use numbers to show impact ✔ Avoid graphics, logos, icons
❌ Never copy-paste an entire JD ❌ Never use text inside tables ❌ Never use images in your resume

Conclusion

Making your resume ATS-friendly is the first step to getting shortlisted. Once your resume passes ATS, your chances of getting an interview increase dramatically.
